⚔️ The Double Cross
The Concept
Every player must play exactly 9 holes from Gold tees and 9 from Red tees — choosing which tee on each hole as they go. Opponents can see your tee choice and play accordingly. That's the Double Cross.
Player Formats
4 players — Team Mode (2v2): Two teams of two. Combined team net score wins the hole. Standard match play tracking (holes up/down).

2–3 players — Individual Mode: Each player competes individually. Lowest individual net score wins the hole. Skins-based — winner collects from each loser. No teams needed.
Tee Allocation
Each player has 9 Gold and 9 Red "credits" across 18 holes. The filled dots show used allocations, empty dots show what remains.
Used   
Remaining    G = Gold
When a player uses all 9 of one tee, the app auto-locks them to the other — shown with a 🟡 Gold only or 🔴 Red only pill.
Scoring Each Hole
1. Each player declares their tee choice (G or R)
2. Play the hole
3. Each player's net score = Gross score − handicap strokes received
4 players: Team A net = Player 1 + Player 2. Lower team net wins.
2–3 players: Lowest individual net wins. Ties = hole halved.
The Double Cross Bonus
When it triggers
The winner played more Gold tees than the loser(s) on that hole — they took the harder, longer tees and still won. Earns a bonus payout on top of the skin.

4 players: Compares team Gold tee count.
2–3 players: Winner played Gold while an opponent played Red.
Example: Player A plays Gold, Player B plays Red, Player A wins → DC Bonus triggered. Both play Gold → no bonus (equal exposure).
Match Conclusion
4 players: Standard match play — a team can win early (e.g. 3&2). Skins continue to the 18th regardless.
2–3 players: All 18 holes are played. Player with most holes won at the end collects the Match Win bonus from each opponent.
🐺 Wolf
The Concept
An individual 4-player game. On each hole, one player is designated the Wolf. After watching everyone tee off, the Wolf decides: pick a partner for a 2v2 that hole, or go Lone Wolf and play 1 vs 3.
Wolf Rotation
The Wolf role rotates in strict order through all 18 holes:
PlayerWolf on holesTotal
P11 · 5 · 9 · 13 · 175×
P22 · 6 · 10 · 14 · 185×
P33 · 7 · 11 · 154×
P44 · 8 · 12 · 164×
Use 🎲 Shuffle Order on the setup screen to randomise who occupies which position — this changes the rotation fairly.
Wolf Decision: Partner Pick
Wolf watches all four drives, then taps a player's name to partner with them. The Wolf + partner play as a team against the other two. Winners each receive +2 pts. Losers receive 0. Halved = 0 all.
Wolf Decision: Lone Wolf 🐺
Wolf plays 1 vs 3. Higher risk, higher reward.

Wolf wins: +4 pts to Wolf, 0 to others
Wolf loses: +2 pts to each of the 3 others, 0 to Wolf
Halved: 0 to all (Wolf ties best individual net score)
Points Summary
2v2 — winning side+2 each
2v2 — losing side0
Lone Wolf wins+4 to Wolf
Lone Wolf loses+2 each to 3 others
Any halved result0 all
Holes 17 & 18 — Comeback Rule
On holes 17 and 18, the player in last place is designated as the Wolf — giving them the option to go Lone Wolf for a 4-point swing. If the last-place player wins hole 17 and is no longer in last, the new last-place player becomes Wolf on hole 18.
Tee Choice in Wolf
In Wolf mode there is no 9/9 tee allocation rule. All players play from the same tee set for the round, selected at setup.
🏆 Nassau
The Concept
Three separate bets in one round: Front 9, Back 9, and Overall 18. Each is an independent match — you can lose the front, win the back, and still come out ahead overall.
Player Formats
4 players — Team Mode (2v2): Teams play match play on each of the three bets. Combined net score wins each hole.

2–3 players — Individual: Each player competes individually. Lowest net score wins each hole. Three independent skins matches.
Scoring
Each hole is won by the lowest net score. Ties = hole halved. The three matches (F9, B9, 18) are tracked separately. A player/team can win, lose, or halve each match independently.
Betting
The Nassau bet amount is paid per match won. With 3 matches, maximum exposure is 3× the Nassau bet. Skins and carry-overs still apply on top of the Nassau bets.
Tee Choice
All players play from the same tee set for the round, selected at setup. No per-hole tee switching (that's Double Cross only).
🔄 Sixes (Round Robin)
The Concept
4 players required. Partners rotate every 6 holes, creating three 2v2 blocks. Each block is a separate match. Every player partners with every other player exactly once.
Partner Rotation
H1–6: Player 1+2 vs Player 3+4
H7–12: Player 1+3 vs Player 2+4
H13–18: Player 1+4 vs Player 2+3
Scoring
Each hole within a block: combined net score of the pair wins the hole. At the end of each 6-hole block, the pair with more holes won collects the Sixes bet from the losing pair. Ties = block halved.
Betting
The Sixes bet is paid per block. With 3 blocks, maximum exposure is 3× the Sixes bet. Skins and carry-overs apply independently across all 18 holes.
💰 The Pot (Stroke Play Tournament)
The Concept
An 18-hole individual stroke play game for 2 or more players. Everyone antes in, and the top finishers split the pot based on the size of the field.

Can be played Gross (raw scores) or Net (with full course handicap applied to level the field).
Scoring Mode
Gross (default): Raw scores count. The best golfer wins.

Net: Full course handicap subtracted from gross total. Levels the field — a 20-handicapper shooting 92 (net 72) beats a scratch golfer shooting 73.
Payout Structure
The pot (ante × number of players) is distributed to top finishers. The default scales automatically by field size, but can be overridden on the Stakes tab. Your payout preference is remembered per course.
Auto (default):
2–4 players: Winner takes all
5–8: Top 2 (65/35)
9–14: Top 3 (50/30/20)
15+: Top 5 (35/25/20/12/8)

Other presets: Top 2, Top 3, Top 5, Top 5 Even Split, Winner Takes All — select on the Stakes tab.
Ties
Tied players split the combined prize money for their positions equally. Example: two players tie for 1st in a 6-player field — they split 1st + 2nd prize (65% + 35% = 100%, so 50% each).
Tee & Handicap
All players play from the same tee set. In Net mode, the course handicap is calculated from the selected tee's CR and Slope. The full handicap is subtracted from the 18-hole gross total.
🤠 Texas Texas Two Low (2-Best-of-4 Foursomes)
The Concept
8 players split into 2 foursomes. On each hole, the 2 lowest gross scores from each foursome are added together. The foursome with the lower combined total wins the hole.
Team Setup
Players are automatically grouped into balanced foursomes using a snake draft by handicap. The app sorts players by skill and alternates picks to create fair teams. You can manually swap players between teams after the draft.
Scoring Each Hole
1. All 8 players play the hole and report gross scores
2. From each foursome, the 2 lowest scores are selected
3. Those 2 scores are added together
4. Lower foursome total wins the hole

Example: Foursome A scores 4, 5, 6, 7 → Two Low = 4+5 = 9
Foursome B scores 4, 4, 7, 8 → Two Low = 4+4 = 8
Foursome B wins the hole.
Carry-Over (Ties)
When both foursomes tie on a hole, the hole's value carries over to the next hole. The next hole is then worth 2 holes. Carries can stack — 3 tied holes in a row means hole 4 is worth 4. Carry-over is unlimited by default but can be capped in settings.
Betting & Settlement
Bet is per player per hole (default $1). With 4 players per team, each hole is worth $4 to the winning team ($1 from each losing player).

At the end of the round, net holes won determines the payout. Example: Team A wins 10 holes, Team B wins 6, 2 ties → Team A wins by 4 net holes → each Team B player pays $4 to the Team A pool ($16 total, split 4 ways = $4 each).
Scoring Mode
Gross only — no handicaps applied. Raw scores determine the two low. This keeps it simple and rewards the best golf on the day.
🦑 Squid Game (3-Player Points)
The Concept
3 players, handicap applies. Win a hole to take the Squid and become the SquidBoss. Hold it and keep winning to build an escalating streak. Points accumulate — lose the Squid and your streak points go to the bank.
1) Squid Shift
Win a hole (lowest net) → take the Squid, become SquidBoss, score 1 point. Tie = Squid doesn't move, no points scored. Lose the Squid = your current streak points are banked (safe).
2) Streak Scoring
Only the SquidBoss scores. Each consecutive win earns escalating points:
1st win = 1 pt · 2nd = 2 pts · 3rd = 3 pts · etc.
Points are "at risk" until banked (via losing the Squid or Stashing).
3) Squid Squirt 💦
If the SquidBoss wins with a gross birdie, all current streak points double immediately. Banked points are not doubled. Streak continues normally after doubling.
4) Squid Snatch 🫳
If a Hunter wins with a gross birdie, they become Insta-Boss and inherit the streak counter. The previous boss banks their points normally. The new boss continues scoring at the next streak level. No Squirting on a Snatch.
5) Squid Stash 💰
Before the next tee, the SquidBoss may bank current streak points. Streak resets to 0. Boss keeps the Squid. Banked points don't double on future Squirts.
6) Squid Attack! ⚔️
On the tee, before any ball is played, a Hunter may call "SQUID ATTACK!"
• If attacker wins the hole: steals the current streak purse + becomes SquidBoss.
• If attacker fails: DOUBLE TROUBLE — streak purse awarded to each of the other two players.
• No Stashing after Attack is called.
7) Squid Mully 🔄
Call after any tee shot. Costs 4 points. No money — no honey!
8) Settlement 💀
Take the lowest score and subtract from everyone (baseline = 0). Each player pays per-point stake for each point difference to each player above them.
Formula: Net payout = 2 × stake × (your pts) − stake × (sum of others' pts)
🧮 Handicap Modes Explained
Both modes use the World Handicap System (WHS) formula for Gold tees. They differ only in how they handle Red tees — specifically whether the handicap changes when a player switches to the shorter tee.
How Strokes Are Awarded (Both Modes)
Your Course Handicap (CHP) is compared against each hole's Stroke Index (SI):

• If your CHP ≥ hole SI → you receive +1 stroke on that hole
• If your CHP ≥ 18 + SI → you receive +2 strokes on that hole
• Your net score = gross score − strokes received
Example: CHP 15, playing hole SI 6 → 15 ≥ 6 ✓ → you get 1 stroke. Your gross 5 becomes net 4.
Mode 1 — Single (Gold HCP)
Formula (Gold only)
CHP = round( HI × (Slope ÷ 113) + (CR − Par) )
Where HI = Handicap Index, Slope = 130, CR = 73.5, Par = 72 for Nansha Mountain Gold.
This CHP is calculated once at setup and applied on every hole regardless of tee played. Whether a player is on Gold or Red, the same number of strokes applies.
Example — HI 10, Mode 1
CHP = round(10 × 129/113 + 73.4 − 72) = round(11.42 + 1.40) = 13
This player receives 1 stroke on all holes with SI 1–13, and 0 strokes on SI 14–18 — on every hole, Gold or Red.
Mode 2 — Adjusted per Tee
Gold Tee
Full WHS formula:
CHP = round(HI × Slope/113 + CR − Par)

Slope 130, CR 73.5, Par 72
Red Tee
Slope-only formula:
CHP = round(HI × Slope/113)

Slope 113, no CR-Par adjustment
The Red tee formula omits the Course Rating correction. This is intentional — on Nansha Mountain, the full WHS Red formula produces a very low CHP (e.g. HI 10 → CHP 7) because the Red CR-Par differential is −4.9. Using slope-only gives a more proportional and intuitive result.

What This Means in Practice
Mode 1 (Single): An HI 10 player gets 1 stroke on all SI 1–13 holes — regardless of whether they chose Gold or Red that hole. Playing Red gives them a natural scoring advantage (shorter hole, same strokes) without any handicap adjustment.

Mode 2 (Adjusted): The same player gets CHP 13 on Gold holes (strokes on SI 1–13) but only CHP 10 on Red holes (strokes on SI 1–10 only). Red removes 3 stroke advantages on holes SI 11, 12 and 13.
Which Mode Should You Use?
Recommendation
Mode 1 (Single) is simpler and traditional. Best when players have similar handicaps or when simplicity matters more than precision.

Mode 2 (Adjusted) is more equitable — players who choose the easier Red tee give up some stroke advantages. This makes the tee choice itself more meaningful and strategic, which suits the Double Cross format well.

Whichever mode you choose, all players use the same mode — it's a round-level setting.

💰 Betting & Action Rules
All bet types run simultaneously and independently. Stakes are set before the round and locked at Tee Off. Currency is configurable (RMB, USD, THB, VND, KRW, HKD).
🏌️ Skins
Per-hole pot — carries over on halved holes
Each hole, each loser pays the Skin Value. The winner(s) split the pot.

4 players (teams): Losing team pays, winning team splits.
2–3 players (individual): Winner collects from each loser directly.
Wolf: Winner/loser determined by Wolf decision (partner or Lone Wolf).

Halved holes → skin carries to next hole and stacks multiplicatively.
Carryover example: Skin = 10. Holes 3–5 halved. Hole 6 won → pot = 4 × 10 = 40 per loser.
🐦 Birdie Bounty
Gross score only — no handicap applied
Any player scoring gross 1-under par triggers a bounty. Independent of hole win/loss.

4 players (teams): Both teammates collect from both opponents.
2–3 players / Wolf: The birdie-maker collects from each other player individually.
🦅 Eagle Extortion
Gross 2-under par or better
Same logic as Birdie Bounty but for eagles (gross 2-under) or better. Typically set at a higher amount. Albatross (3-under) also triggers this.
⚔️ DC Bonus
Rewarding the harder tee choice
Triggers when the winner played more Gold tees than the loser(s) on that hole. Extra payout on top of the skin.

4 players: Compares team Gold count. Winning team had more → bonus.
2–3 players: Winner played Gold, an opponent played Red → bonus.
🏆 Match Win
4 players: Team match play — can be won early (e.g. 3&2). Each loser pays the Match Win amount.
2–3 players: Player with the most holes won after 18 collects from each opponent.
Settlement — Who Owes Whom
The 💰 Action Ledger calculates each player's running balance across all bet types. The Settle Up section uses a minimum-transaction algorithm to find the fewest payments needed to zero all balances.

Example: Player A owes Player B 80, Player C owes Player A 40 → app consolidates to: C → B 40, saving one transaction.
